COOKIES POLICY

At TurtleCI, we use cookies and similar technologies to enhance the functionality of our website and provide a better user experience. By using our website, you agree to our use of cookies as described in this policy.

‍

Types of Cookies We Use

1. Session Cookies:
These cookies are temporary and are stored only for the duration of your visit to the TurtleCI website. They are deleted once you close your browser.

‍
2. Permanent Cookies:
These cookies are stored on your device for a specific period or until you manually delete them. They help remember your preferences for future visits to our site.

‍
3. Necessary Cookies:
These cookies are essential for the basic functions of the website, such as enabling you to sign in or navigate the site. Without them, certain features may not be available.

‍
4. Security Cookies:
These cookies provide security features, such as detecting fraud during authentication processes and ensuring that our services remain secure.

‍
5. Performance Cookies:
These cookies collect information about how users interact with the website. This data helps us analyze and improve website functionality, user experience, and performance.

‍
6. Functionality or Preferences Cookies:
These cookies remember your settings, such as language, region, font size, and other preferences, to provide a more personalized browsing experience.

‍
7. Analytics Cookies:
These cookies help us track and analyze user behavior, which allows us to improve our services and understand how users interact with our website.

‍
8. Advertising Cookies:
These cookies are used to display relevant and targeted advertisements to you. They also allow us to measure the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns.
‍

Third-Party Cookies:

In addition to the cookies we use, third-party cookies may be placed on your device by external services. These may come from platforms or services such as social media, analytics tools, or advertising providers. These cookies may track your interactions with our website as well as other websites you visit.

‍
Examples of third-party cookies include:

  • Cookies from social media platforms (e.g., Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn)
  • Analytics cookies from tools like Google Analytics and Hotjar
  • Advertising cookies from platforms such as Facebook Pixel

‍

Managing Cookies

‍You can manage or delete cookies at any time by adjusting your browser settings. Most browsers are set to accept cookies automatically, but you can configure your settings to:

  • Block cookies entirely
  • Prompt you to accept cookies before they are stored
  • Delete cookies already stored on your device

Please note that blocking or deleting cookies may affect your experience on the website and limit the functionality of certain features.
